8 Guidelines for Safe Dating


It is the perpetrator, not the woman, who causes the rape by an acquaintance. However, there are certain things that women can do on their own to reduce the risk of damage. In this regard, we introduce the "guidelines for beliefs and behaviors that women should adopt" presented by sexual violence counselors and expert groups.

1. You have the right to set your own sexual limits and communicate with your opponent.


Set your limits and clarify them to the other person before performing sexual behavior. Most men don't know what you want because they're not psychic. If you let the other person guess what you want, you can eventually get hurt.

Whatever sexual behavior, including sex, should be determined by communication between the two. Just because you say you're not interested in sex doesn't mean it's rude, and expressing your interest in sex doesn't mean that you're not a woman. It's not wrong at all whether you just want a kiss, or just allow a kiss and a pat on the chest, or anything else without an insert.

If you put a limit on sexual behavior and your opponent ignores it, act immediately. In this situation, you should clearly tell the opponent what you are against. You can speak friendly yet firmly. If you do so and your opponent continues to ignore your principles, get angry. Don't worry about being polite. If you have to shout or hurt someone else to get help or run away, feel free to do so. Forget about being a 'fine woman'. Don't worry about how the other person will feel and make a fuss.

4. Find out about your new date.


Don't leave a party place (MT place or anywhere) with someone you just met, someone you don't know well. Also, if you start dating, you'd better learn more about the person by meeting with friends the first few times or by getting information from the woman you met before. 5. Keep in control.


Pay for yourself so that dating doesn't look like a deal and it doesn't translate into something "debt" to you. (Or if the other person buys a movie ticket, split it up the way you buy pizza later.)

Always carry plenty of cash with you so you can take a taxi home alone. And when the man you just met offers you a ride, don't accept it.

6. Take care of yourself.


Don't think other people will care about you or protect you from danger. Instead, hold a self-defense program in advance in case you need it. In most areas, these programs (programs develop the ability to recognize dangerous situations, act decisively, avoid attacks and respond strongly).
